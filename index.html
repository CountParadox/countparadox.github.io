Introduction

I first became interested in radio long ago, but always thought it was far out of my league of what I could afford. Then one day a few years ago I realised chinese radios like the Baofeng UV-5R existed, I promptly began studying for my Foudnation license and was able to get it within the following weeks. Since then I have upgraded my gear, learnt so much more, and begun developing my own hardware and doing experiments. Check out my blog for active projects im working on, or give this site a read for some general reference material.

Allowed Bands

Australian Foudnation Lincence Band Plan

  • 80m3.500MHz-3.700MHz
  • 40m7.000MHz-7.300MHz
  • 15m21.000MHz-21.450MHz
  • 10m28.000MHz-29.700MHz
  • 2m144MHz-148MHz
  • 70cm430MHz-450MHz

Foudnation Licence class has a maximum output power of 10w. They may operate in any mode, with commercial or self-built hardware and may allow non-licensed people to operate an amateur radio under their supervision.

Radio Hardware

I operate mainly portable with a Yaesu FT-3DR with a Diamond SRH701. I am usually monitoring on VK2RBV (438.1125Mhz) in both C4FM/YSF Digital and FM.
I really enjoy building antenna! I have many different types, from 1/4 wave ground planes, to yagi, and even some really well build dipoles!

At home I have a Yaesu FT-897D, I also take this out with me when operating portable HF. I have a Youkits TJ2B for HF portable as well, with an MFJ-1899T for QRP operation.
